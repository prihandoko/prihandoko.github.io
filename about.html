Rudi Prihandoko
Writer, thinker, occasional mathematician.

I write about the things I can't stop thinking about — mathematics, language, the texture of ideas, and what it means to pay attention. This blog is less a publication and more a notebook made public.

By day I work on things that involve words and people. By night I read widely and take notes I sometimes turn into essays. I am interested in beauty in unexpected places: a proof, a sentence, a well-placed silence.

I grew up believing that disciplines had walls. I no longer believe that. The best thinking I've encountered lives at the edges — where a philosopher borrows from physics, where a novelist thinks like a mathematician, where rigor and intuition stop arguing and start collaborating.

If something here resonates, I'd be glad to hear from you.
